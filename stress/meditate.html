What is Meditation?

Meditation is on path towards transforming the mind. Buddhist meditation practices encourage and develop concentration, clarity, and emotional positivity. When you begin a meditation practice, you may learn the patterns and habits of your mind. Meditation offers you a way to cultivate new and more positive ways to approach the things you do throughout your day. Meditation can have a transformative effect and can lead to positive changes as well as a new understanding of life.

Why Meditate?

  • Be Happy: meditation causes the pituitary gland in the brain to secrete endorphins that help elevate mood and have a positive effective on the whole body.
  • Reduce stress: endorphins also help reduce stress levels in the brain.
  • Focus: through meditation you can learn self-discipline so your mind can focus with increased efficency.
  • Sleep better: Scientific studies have shows that those who meditate enhance their slow wave sleep pattern which can help fight insomnia.
  • Lower blood pressure: research has also shows that meditation can reduce blood pressure both in the short term and later in life.
  • Pain relief: the long term practice of meditation can lead to physical changes in the brain that help alter the perception of pain.
